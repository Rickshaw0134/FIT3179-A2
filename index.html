Malaysia's Population: Trends, Diversity & Growth

Malaysia, located in Southeast Asia, has experienced significant population growth recently. Currently, the country’s population is around 33 million, as of 2023. This growth has been driven by increasing urbanization, improved healthcare, and rising economic development. Although the population growth rate is slowing, it is still projected to continue in the coming years. By 2030, Malaysia’s population could reach nearly 37 million people, driven by both natural increase and migration. However, the country also faces challenges, especially as its workforce ages rapidly over time. Therefore, sustaining economic growth and managing resources will be essential to support this growing population.

Population Density by State & State Population across the years

Malaysia’s population density shows notable variation across different states. The western region, particularly Selangor and Kuala Lumpur, has the highest density. This is indicated by the darker blue shades on the map. In contrast, the eastern states, such as Sabah and Sarawak, have much lower density. These states are represented by lighter green colors.

The heatmap below also illustrates the absolute population by state and year. Selangor, Johor, and Perak consistently show large populations, seen in the darker boxes.
